About

I am Shao-Heng Ko (柯劭珩), currently a research assistant at Institute of Information Science (IIS), Academia Sinica under the supervision of Dr. De-Nian Yang. I received my B.S. (2011-2015) and M.S. (2015-2017) degrees in Department/Graduate Institute of Electrical Engineering at National Taiwan University (NTU), where I was very fortunate to have Professor Ho-Lin Chen as my advisor and also Professor I-Hsiang Wang as my undergraduate research advisor.

My research interests are algorithms and game theory/mechanism design. I am generally interested in all kinds of algorithms: exact, approximation, randomized, streaming, online, and distributed/parallel ones. At Academia Sinica, I design approximation algorithms for novel roblems with applications in Virtual Reality, marketing, social network, etc.

Outside of computer science, I am also interested in Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs). I was a MOOC explorer in Lab of Teaching Innovation (now part of the Digital Learning Center) at NTU from 2015-2017, where I did a lot of things around MOOCs, including studying, reviewing and even creating them. I am also casually interested in Transportation and Urban Planning.

I love playing baseball, watching baseball and also Sabermetrics/baseball statistics. Playing baseball is an essential part in my life. I also play a little handball and basketball. Moreover, I like playing board games; some of my favorites are Tickets to Ride and Betrayal at House on the Hill.

I was born in the beautiful harbor city of Kaohsiung. I am also a descendant of the even-more-beautiful island of Ji-Bei, the northernmost inhabited island among the Penghu Islands, famous for its sand beach.

About

Hi! I am Shao-Heng Ko (柯劭珩),

a first-year PhD student at Theory Group at Duke University.

Until recently (2017-2020.7), I was a research assistant at Institute of Information Science (IIS), Academia Sinica, Taiwan, supervised by Dr. De-Nian Yang. Prior to that, I received my M.S. (2015-2017) and B.S. (2011-2015) in Electrical Engineering from National Taiwan University (NTU), where I was very fortunate to be advised by Dr. Ho-Lin Chen.

I am broadly interested in (discrete) algorithms and their applications. My past research involves applying algorithmic tools to solve novel arising problems in Virtual Reality, e-commerce, social network, etc. I am also interested in algorithmic game theory, algorithmic fairness, and mechanism design.

Outside of academics, I am a believer of Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and also an enthusiastic baseball fan and player. I was born in the beautiful harbor city of Kaohsiung, Taiwan. I am also a descendant of the even-more-beautiful island of Ji-Bei, the northernmost inhabited island among the Penghu Islands.

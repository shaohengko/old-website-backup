About

Hi! I am Shao-Heng Ko (柯劭珩),

currently a research assistant at Institute of Information Science (IIS), Academia Sinica under the supervision of Dr. De-Nian Yang. I will soon start my PhD studies at Theory Group at Duke University.

I received my B.S. (2011-2015) and M.S. (2015-2017) degrees in Department/Graduate Institute of Electrical Engineering at National Taiwan University (NTU), where I was very fortunate to have Professor Ho-Lin Chen as my advisor and also Professor I-Hsiang Wang as my undergraduate research advisor.

I am broadly interested in all kinds of (mostly) discrete algorithms and their applications. I am also interested in algorithmic game theory, mechanism design, and computational economics. My past research works at Academia Sinica mainly involves designing approximation algorithms for database problems with novel applications in Virtual Reality, marketing, social network, etc. Please see the research tab for more details.

Outside of computer science, I am a believer of Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs). I was a MOOC explorer at the Lab of Teaching Innovation at NTU from 2015-2017. If you wonder what that means, check out the MOOCs tab on the left.

I am a serious baseball fan. I LOVE playing baseball, watching baseball and also Sabermetrics/baseball statistics. Check out the Baseball tab for Shao-Heng the baseball player! My other interests are board games, transportation, and urban planning.

I was born in the beautiful harbor city of Kaohsiung. I am also a descendant of the even-more-beautiful island of Ji-Bei, the northernmost inhabited island among the Penghu Islands, famous for its sand beach.

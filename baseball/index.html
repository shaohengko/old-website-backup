Baseball

Baseball is a daily part of my life. I enjoy playing it, watching it, talking about it, and also those geeky sabermetric analysis articles.

During my high school life, I was a part of the varsity team of Kaohsiung Municipal High School (the team can date back to the Japanese colonial period, i.e., 1920s), where I was primarily a first baseman but occasionally played other infield positions. Baseball colored my high school life! I was very fortunate to meet a bunch of good buddies there, including that geek who made me join the team: Justin T. Hou, now at MIT EECS.

Many of my buddies went to NTU. We remain close as family during college life. Enjoying playing together so much, we founded our own team, NTU KSBC (meaning: KSHS alumni players at NTU), the first high-school alumni-based baseball team at NTU. We won four consequetive championships in tournaments, including a 27-game winning streak spanning 2.5 years. A commemorative album of our story can be found here.

Gifted with good arm strength, I began to pitch in college. Starting from absorbing irrelevant innings in my sophomore year, I made great strides and was starting semi-final games in the later years. Since we always played short-handed, I was also plugged to whatever position with a need (guess my defense was decent), appearing 9 times at LF, 6 times at CF, 6 times at RF, 3 times at 3B, 10 times at 1B, and 18 times on the mound. Across my time at KSBC, I owned a .315/.465/.407 slash line and a 4.50 ERA7 with a record of 8-0 (which really speaks more about the talents of my teammates). Kudos to them, including fellow TCS guy Chi-Ning Chou, whom I stole this website template from. Thanks Chi-Ning!

We disbanded in 2016 due to players graduating. I continued playing and joined SSWinds (team of the Sociology and Social Works) where I met my teammate Chih-Chiang Chen (CityWall), a famous baseball writer (and also a diehard Minnesota Twins fan) in Taiwan.

In my last year of MS, I was elected into the very first NTU All-star game, closing the game for the red team (1.0IP, 1K, 0R). During that year, I debuted as a catcher and also played some SS and 2B for SSwinds, completing the accomplishment of having played every position on the field.

In the years outside school, I pitched for the NTU Economics OB team in the Holiday Baseball League, Taipei (this is irrelevant with my interest in game theory, btw) from 2017 to 2020, mainly as a reliever and a third baseman. My recent pitching line in 2020: 1-2 in 8 games, 23.2IP, 32K, 19BB, 12ER, ERA:4.56. My repertoire includes a fastball that tops at 80-81 mph, a slider, a changeup, and an eephus that I use from time to time for recreational purposes.

I started watching MLB back to 2008 and I root for the Tampa Bay Rays (and of course, the Durham Bulls, as a consequence). Their underdog mentality and their radical and creative mindset align well with my philosophy.

Baseball

Baseball is a daily part of my life. I enjoy playing it, watching it, talking about it and also a little bit of research on it. Having a father who played in colleges and also an uncle as a baseball coach at local high schools and colleges definitely helps.

The first real baseball team I joined was the varsity team of Kaohsiung Municipal High School (KSHS), whose existence could be dated back to the Japanese colonial period (1922), where I was primarily a first baseman but occasionally appeared at other infield positions. Baseball colored my next-to-boring high school life, and I was very fortunate to meet a bunch of good buddies there, including that guy who invited me into the team: Justin T. Hou, now at MIT EECS. (Hope he creates his page soon such that I could link to him here!)

Many of those buddies were admitted into NTU where we supported each other during college life. Enjoying playing baseball together so much, we founded our own team, NTU KSBC (reads: former KSHS players at NTU), essentially the first high-school alumni-based baseball team at NTU. We won four consequetive championships in tournaments, including a 27-game winning streak spanning two and a half years. A commemorative album of our story can be found here.

Gifted with good arm strength, and a lackluster bat, I started pitching seriously, and started working as a reliever in the sophomore year, finishing some games for Justin the ace. Since we always played short-handed, I also became a utility player plugged to whatever position with a need. In total, I appeared 9 times at LF, 6 times at CF, 6 times at RF, 3 times at 3B, 10 times at 1B, and 18 times on the mound. I ended up being decent at almost everything except catching, and my bat finally came to life in my senior year. I owned a .315/.465/.407 triple-slash line and a 4.50 ERA7 with a record of 8-0. Kudos to my teammates, including Chi-Ning Chou, whom I stole this website template from. Thanks Chi-Ning!

We disbanded in 2016 due to players graduating. I continued playing and joined SSWinds (team of the Sociology and Social Works) where I met my teammate Chih-Chiang Chen (CityWall), now a famous amateur baseball writer (and also a diehard Minnesota Twins fan) in Taiwan.

In my last year as a graduate student, I was elected into the 1st NTU All-star game, pitching for the red team (1.0IP, 1K, 0R) in a 8-8 tie. I also caught for the first time (and also caught a runner stealing!) that year. I appeared at 2B in my last official game at NTU, completing the accomplishment of appearing in every position.

After graduation, I pitched for the NTU Economics OB team in the Holiday Baseball League (this has nothing to do with my interest in game theory) from 2017 until now. My recent pitching line in 2019: 0-3, 4SV in 15 games, 44.0IP, 56K, 37BB, 21ER, ERA7:3.341, WHIP:1.73, opponent AVG:0.217. My repertoire includes a fastball that tops at 80-81 mph, a slider, a changeup and an eephus pitch that I use from time to time for recreational purposes.

I started watching MLB back to 2008 and I root for the Tampa Bay Rays. Their underdog mentality and their radical and creative mindset align well with my philosophy.
